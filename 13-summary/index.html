Core Principles

Superposition

Qubits can exist in multiple states simultaneously, enabling quantum parallelism and exponential computational power.

Entanglement

Quantum correlations between qubits that are stronger than any classical correlation, enabling quantum communication and cryptography.

Interference

Quantum amplitudes can interfere constructively or destructively, allowing quantum algorithms to amplify correct answers.

Measurement

Observing a quantum state collapses it to a classical outcome, with probabilities determined by quantum amplitudes.

Unitarity

Quantum evolution is reversible and preserves probability, governed by unitary operators.

No-Cloning

Quantum states cannot be copied, a fundamental limitation with important implications for quantum information.

Quantum Gates and Operations

Single-Qubit Gates

  • Pauli Gates (X, Y, Z): Bit flip, combined flip, phase flip
  • Hadamard (H): Creates superposition, basis change
  • Phase Gates (S, T): Add quantum phase
  • Rotation Gates (Rx, Ry, Rz): Arbitrary rotations on Bloch sphere

Two-Qubit Gates

  • CNOT: Controlled-NOT, creates entanglement
  • CZ: Controlled-Z, phase gate
  • SWAP: Exchanges qubit states
  • Toffoli: Three-qubit controlled-controlled-NOT

üîë Universal Gate Sets

Any quantum computation can be decomposed into a sequence of single-qubit rotations and CNOT gates. Common universal sets: {H, T, CNOT} or {Rx, Ry, Rz, CNOT}.

Physical Implementations

Leading Technologies

  • Superconducting Qubits: IBM, Google, Rigetti - Fast gates, cryogenic temperatures
  • Trapped Ions: IonQ, Honeywell - High fidelity, slower gates
  • Photonic Qubits: Xanadu, PsiQuantum - Room temperature, networking
  • Neutral Atoms: QuEra, Pasqal - Scalable arrays
  • Topological Qubits: Microsoft - Error-resistant (experimental)

Key Challenges

  • Decoherence and noise
  • Gate errors and fidelity
  • Scalability to many qubits
  • Connectivity and routing
  • Error correction overhead

Quantum Algorithms

Major Algorithms

  • Shor's Algorithm: Factor integers in polynomial time (exponential speedup)
  • Grover's Algorithm: Search unsorted database in O(‚àöN) time (quadratic speedup)
  • Quantum Simulation: Simulate quantum systems efficiently
  • VQE: Variational quantum eigensolver for chemistry
  • QAOA: Quantum approximate optimization algorithm
  • HHL: Solve linear systems exponentially faster

Applications

  • Cryptography: Breaking RSA, quantum key distribution
  • Chemistry: Molecular simulation, drug discovery
  • Optimization: Logistics, finance, machine learning
  • Materials Science: Design new materials
  • Artificial Intelligence: Quantum machine learning

  • No-Cloning: Cannot copy arbitrary quantum states
  • No-Deleting: Cannot delete quantum information
  • No-Communication: Entanglement alone cannot transmit information
  • Uncertainty Principle: Cannot measure non-commuting observables simultaneously

Current State and Future

Where We Are (2024)

  • ~1000 qubit systems demonstrated
  • Quantum advantage for specific problems
  • NISQ era: Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum
  • Active research in error correction
  • Growing quantum software ecosystem

Path Forward

  • Near-term (1-5 years): Improved NISQ algorithms, better hardware
  • Medium-term (5-10 years): Logical qubits, small error-corrected systems
  • Long-term (10+ years): Fault-tolerant quantum computers, broad applications
